Permutation matrices play a crucial role in linear algebra, particularly in the context of matrix operations. A permutation matrix is a square binary matrix that represents a specific rearrangement of the columns (or rows) of the identity matrix. Each row and each column contain exactly one entry of 1, with all other entries being 0.
