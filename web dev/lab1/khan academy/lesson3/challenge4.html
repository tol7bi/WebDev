Recipe #1

  • Time: 1 hour
  • Serves: Mayonnaise , dill
For filling:
Ingredients Quantity
Minced meat 700g
Onion 1 pc
Garlic 2 cloves
Dill greens 1 small bunch
Ground black pepper to taste
Salt to taste
For the dough:
Flour 3 cups
Egg 1 pc
Vegetable oil 3 tablespoons
Salt 0.5 teaspoons
Boiling water 1 cup

Step 1: Shake the egg with salt, add vegetable oil and 3 cups of flour, mix. Then pour in a glass of boiling water and quickly mix the dough with a spoon. After that, put the dough on the table and knead with your hands for 5 minutes (if necessary, you can add flour). The dough turns out to be soft, elastic and does not stick to your hands at all. Put the dough in a bag and leave for 30 minutes.

Step 2: Season the minced meat with pepper, salt, add chopped herbs and chopped onion and garlic in a blender. Mix well, beat off and put in the refrigerator for half an hour.

Step 3: Cut off a small part of the finished dough (put the remaining piece back into the bag), roll up the tourniquet, cut it into pieces.

Step 4: Knead the dough pieces with your fingers and then roll each out with a rolling pin in the shape of a circle.Put a little minced meat on each circle of dough and make dumplings of the shape that you like. Place on a floured tray or board.

Step 5: Boil the required amount of dumplings in salted, boiling water with the addition of a bay leaf until ready (after surfacing – about 5 minutes). Freeze the rest of the dumplings.

Step 6:Serve ready-made dumplings with butter, sour cream or vinegar mixed with herbs and garlic. Enjoy your meal!

Recipe #2

Chicken soup

  • Time: 50 min
  • Serves: cheese scone
Ingredients Quantity
Olive oil 1 tbsp
Onion 2 pc,chopped
Carrots 3 pc,chopped
Thyme leaves 1 tbsp
Chicken stock 1.4l
Leftover roast chicken 300g
Frozen peas 200g
Greek yogurt 3 tbsp
Squeeze of lemon juice 1 l
cheese scone -

Step 1: Heat the olive oil in a large heavy-based pan. Add the onions, carrots and thyme leaves, then gently fry for 15 mins.

Step 2:Stir in 1.4l chicken stock, bring to a boil, cover, then simmer for 10 mins.

Step 3: Add the leftover roast chicken, remove half the mixture, then purée with a stick blender. Tip back into the pan with the rest of the soup, the peas and seasoning, then simmer for 5 mins until hot through.

Step 4:Mix the Greek yogurt, garlic and lemon juice together. Ladle the soup into bowls, swirl in the garlic yogurt, then serve.

Step 5: If you want to use a slow cooker, gently fry the onions, carrots and thyme leaves for 15 mins, then tip them into the slow cooker with 1 litre stock. If you're using a chicken carcass, add it now. Cover and cook for 2-3 hours on High until the veg is tender. If you used a carcass, remove it now, shredding any remaining chicken from the bones. Stir back into the soup, or add the leftover roast chicken and frozen peas. Cook for 30 mins more. Remove half the mixture and purée with a stick blender, then serve as above.
